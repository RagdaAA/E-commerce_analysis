# TechNet

## Company Overview
TechNet is a global direct-to-consumer e-commerce company specializing in consumer electronics, including computers, mobile phones, and audio accessories. The company sells exclusively through its online platform across four regions, allowing full control over pricing, promotions, and customer engagement.

As a data analyst at TechNet, this analysis was conducted to help business stakeholders understand how sales performance and customer behavior have evolved over time, particularly during and after the COVID-19 pandemic. By examining historical sales data from 2019 to 2022, the goal is to identify key opportunities to drive sustainable revenue growth in a post-pandemic market.

## Insights Deep-Dive
### Overall trends
Analysis of sales and order count over time shows a consistent seasonal pattern across all years. Sales and order volume typically decline between Q3 and Q4 followed by a recovery. The strongest increase occurring towards Q1-Q2. Notably, this seasonal pattern persists even during the sales surge observed in 2020, indicating that the dips are structural rather than event-driven. 

#### Product Performance
Across all years the four products driving the sales numbers were the Gaming Monitor, Apple AirPods, MacBook and ThinkPad Laptop. On the other hand, Bose headphones, iPhone, and Webcam consistently rank lowest in revenue. In terms of order volume Apple AirPods, Gaming Monitor and Charging cable rank the highest while ThinkPad, iPhone, and Bose trail behind. Regarding AOV, MacBook, ThinkPad, and iPhone rank highest, whereas Charging Cable, Webcam, and Bose headphones rank lowest. Taken together, Bose Headphones and Apple iPhone underperform across sales and order count, suggesting they are not central to revenue generation.


#### Regions
North America dominates TechNet’s performance, generating approximately 50% of total revenue and the highest order volume. However, APAC records the highest AOV, followed by North America, suggesting stronger high-value purchasing behaviour in these two regions. LATAM underperforms significantly, contributing around 6% of total revenue.

Across regions, the same four products; MacBook, ThinkPad, AirPods, and Gaming Monitor, consistently lead sales. Sales trends over time show similar seasonal patterns across all regions, with no region overtaking another during peak periods, indicating shared global seasonality.

### Loyalty program
At the end of 2019 loyalty and non-loyalty customers exhibited similar AOV, while non-loyalty customers placed significantly more orders. This suggests that loyalty customers were purchasing higher-priced items despite lower order volume. However, this pattern was short-lived and did not persist into 2020. Instead the AOV for loyalty customers stayed relatively consistent throughout 2020 and into 2022. On the otherhand, the surge in overall sales observed in 2020 was primarily driven by non-loyalty customers, indicating that growth during this period was largely acquisition-based rather than retention-driven. 

From 2021 onward, loyalty customers consistently outperformed non-loyalty customers in both order count and AOV. This pattern indicates stronger retention and purchasing behaviour among loyalty members, even during periods of declining overall sales. An exception occurs in the final quarter of 2022, where loyalty customer activity declined.

Overall, the loyalty program has contributed positively to sales performance for over two years and should be maintained, with targeted marketing to sustain engagement.


### Refunds
The data shows that refund activity increases in line with higher sales volumes, while overall refund levels remain relatively low over time. However, refunded orders tend to have a higher-than-average AOV, indicating that returns disproportionately impact higher-value purchases. The highest refund rates are observed for ThinkPad (16.2%), MacBook (16.0%), and iPhone (9.8%). ThinkPad is particularly notable: despite being among the higher-selling products, it also has the highest refund rate across both loyalty and non-loyalty customers.

North America consistently shows the highest refunds across the observed period. In 2019 and 2020, NA recorded refund rates of approximately 3% and 5%, respectively, making it the leading region in terms of returns. In 2021, refund rates declined, with NA and EMEA both reporting similar levels of around 2%. However, it is important to note that no refunds were recorded after July 2021, meaning that the 2021 figures are based only on partial-year data and should be interpreted with caution.

In terms of overall volume, NA accounts for a disproportionately large share of total returns, representing approximately 53–58% of all refunds across all years. The remaining returns are distributed across APAC, EMEA, and LATAM combined.


## Recommendations
### Overall trends
The recurring seasonal dips in sales and order volume indicate predictable periods of lower demand throughout the year. To mitigate these declines, it is recommended to implement proactive, time-targeted marketing campaigns ahead of these periods. Examples include promotional offers, personalized outreach, or limited-time incentives designed to sustain customer engagement and stabilize revenue.

#### Products
The Bose product line consistently underperforms across key metrics, including sales, average order value (AOV), and order volume. Based on this, it is recommended to reassess the role of Bose products in the portfolio, with consideration given to discontinuation or replacement with higher-performing alternatives.

#### Regions
LATAM contributes a relatively small share of total revenue and ranks lowest in both order volume and AOV. This raises questions about the efficiency of continued investment in this region. It is recommended to re-evaluate the current market strategy for LATAM, including distribution, marketing spend, and product-market fit, to determine whether adjustments or resource reallocation are warranted.

### Loyalty program
The loyalty program has demonstrated strong performance, particularly from 2021 onward. However, several opportunities for optimization remain:

The temporary increase in high-value purchases among loyalty customers in Q4 2019 should be further analyzed to identify which marketing activities or campaigns drove this behavior, with the goal of replicating similar outcomes.
The decline in loyalty customer activity in Q4 2022 indicates a need for renewed engagement strategies, such as targeted campaigns or incentives to maintain retention.
Data shows that loyalty customers are more likely to purchase lower AOV products repeatedly, suggesting strong retention behavior. Therefore, it is recommended to focus on converting one-time buyers of high-AOV products into loyalty members, as this could increase the likelihood of repeat purchases and long-term customer value.

### Refunds
Refunds represent a significant risk to revenue, particularly for high-AOV products. The following actions are recommended:

Investigate whether high refund rates for products such as ThinkPad (~16.2%), MacBook (~16.0%), and iPhone (~9.8%) are linked to specific suppliers, product quality issues, or mismatched customer expectations.
Analyze the absence of recorded refunds after July 2021, as this may indicate data quality issues, system errors, or delays in refund processing.
Further examine the consistently high concentration of returns in North America (NA). This pattern suggests potential region-specific factors, such as differences in customer behavior, return policies, fulfillment experience, or product-market fit, which should be explored in more detail.
